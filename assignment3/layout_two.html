Jstn Wins MVP

The veteran Jstn brings in his first MVP award in the inagural season for Twin Cities Esports League. Racking up 22 goals and 15 assists makes it no question who was gonna win. Jstn is keeping the same teammates in TNobes and Jelder, these three already showing their dominance as season two is into its first month. After three games this Bloomington team has rolled through their opponents only giving up 3 goals in toatal.

Season 1 Finals Come to an end

Nobody knew how the inagural season for Twin Cities Esports was going to turn out. After a lively crowd at the Xcel Energy Center for the Season 1 finals, we can tell this league is here to stay. The final 4 style event was between Bloomington, Minneapolis, Edina, and Duluth. The Bloomington squad was heavily favoured coming into the event, and to the surprise of no one Bloomington brought home the Championship! After rolling through Duluth in the semifinal, Minneapolis put up quite the fight in the championship, bringing it to game 7.

St. Paul is out for Season 2

After an embarassing season 1 showing from the St. Paul team, they are out for season 2. When you score only 2 goals the whole season, with one being an own goal by the opponent. It's not very surprising that they were not invited back. The problem they ran into was their offensive ability. The defense was better than most teamss above them, this being in part by GarretG who was spectacular last season when he lead the league in saves. Taking their place will be Duluth Kraken, they're already doing better than St. Paul last season witha record of 2-1.
